# $SGL Staking

Staking `$SGL` puts skin-in-the-game behind the Singularity compute network. Stakers earn a share of network revenue, and staking is what lets you **run a compute node**, **validate the marketplace**, or simply **earn yield**.

- **Live on Solana mainnet** — program `3zChiHHFxbT3qQJBrwyQgTohfX8jS91bUGT8gpyB7aBC`
- **Token** — `$SGL` mint `5c4HyD2rSShqnTsf5z3SaoD2H3GE452u2CUuYjviBAGS` (9 decimals)
- **Rewards** — paid in **USDC + $SGL** (stakers earn 10% of compute revenue)
- **Minimum** — 50,000 $SGL per position

## Roles (stake types)

| Role | Use | Cooldown |
|------|-----|----------|
| `compute` | Run a compute node on the grid | 1 day |
| `validator` | Validate marketplace / ERC-8004 | 2 days |
| `yield` | Earn rewards only | 12 hours |

Each wallet has one position per role. Unstaking starts a cooldown; after it elapses you withdraw.

## How rewards work

Network compute revenue is split **80% operator / 10% platform / 5% stakers / 5% burn**. The staker share accrues to your position in USDC (and $SGL), claimable any time while your position is `active`.

## Slashing & off-grid

Slashing is reserved for **tampering** (a node serving manipulated results) — not for being offline. Compute operators who need planned downtime can flip their node **off-grid** (maintenance) from the dashboard or CLI: it's removed from job dispatch and never penalized for being offline. See [Run a node](https://cloud.x402compute.cc).
